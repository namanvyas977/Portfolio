A Brief Summary

During my research internship at SYSIdea Robotics Lab in IIT Gandhinagar, I took on a project to create a robotics actuator. At first, I used a common DC brushed motor available in the market and designed it with the help of SolidWorks, a mechanical design software. While working on this, I also supported my fellow lab members with their projects. In the initial stages, I used a 3D printer to check dimensions and test an AS5600 encoder. After successful testing, I moved on to aluminum machining, creating a prototype. However, this first version turned out to be too heavy and big for real-world use. So, I worked on a second version, making it more compact and lightweight, which improved the design.

Later on, I took on the challenge of designing another actuator, this time using a BLDC motor. This involved integrating the BLDC motor with an RLX absolute encoder, a gear reduction box, a pre-made motor controller circuit, and an important part called a series elastic element. This required designing a series elastic actuator from scratch. While I didn't have time to manufacture it, I successfully completed the design and checked dimensions using an FDM 3D printer. This whole experience not only taught me a lot about robotics actuator design but also improved my understanding of electrical machines and expanded my skills.
