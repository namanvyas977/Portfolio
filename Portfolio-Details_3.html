A Brief Summary

In my second year, I continued my involvement in the GTU Robotics Club and took on the role of the leader for the robot designing and calculation sub-team. The challenge for that year's Robocon theme required us to build two robots capable of picking arrows from a predefined rack and the ground, throwing them to targeted ports, and passing arrows to the second robot. Both robots featured a 4 omni-wheel configuration, enabling easy movement in all directions without changing their front face. The calculations involved the application of aerodynamics and projectile motion concepts.

Our team's efforts led to notable achievements, with a 1st runner-up position in the National Robocon 2021 and securing the 9th rank in the ABU International Robocon 2021. These successes underscored the effectiveness of our robot designs and calculations, showcasing the team's dedication and skill in the field of robotics.
