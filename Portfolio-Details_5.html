Exploring the World of Battery Pack Engineering: My Journey in the Proto Lab


As a Graduate Engineer Trainee at the Proto Lab in Exide Energy, I've had the privilege of working on some fascinating projects that have challenged and inspired me every step of the way.


Project 1: EastPenn Battery Packs (51.2V, 16Ah) - UL Certification

Our first project is focused on creating battery packs that meet stringent reliability standards. These packs, used in stationary applications. Making these packs has been both challenging and rewarding. We've manufactured numerous packs following rigorous protocols, often putting in long hours, including weekends and holidays. Despite the hard work, the experience has been invaluable, allowing us to hone our skills and become proficient in our craft.

We have sent sevral battery packs to UL Laboratory for their testing. and After reciving the UL Cerification from proto lab we have sent 20 battery packs to the Customer.

It gives me immense pleasure that the battery packs which, i along with the proto team, assembled the packs and tested are working smoothly and gives the proper power backups to the server rooms.


Project 2: Telecom Battery Packs (48V, 100Ah)

Our first project focuses on testing battery packs used in telecom applications. Recently, we've been testing the foam between the cells to make sure they're just right. This ensures that the packs perform well and are safe to use.

We've successfully finished the initial stage of Proto building the battery packs and are now moving forward to the next phase which is pilot phase. we've conducted extensive tests to ensure optimal performance under various conditions, including extreme scenarios like short circuits and overheating.

Over the past 10 months, my journey has been akin to a roller coaster ride, filled with ups and downs. Throughout this time.I've delved deeply into the world of lithium-ion batteries, gaining insights into their assembly, Battery Management Systems (BMS), DC supply, cabling, and more. These technical learnings have been complemented by the development of crucial non-technical skills, such as effective communication, project management, taking ownership, and leadership. However, my learning journey doesn't stop here. Every day presents new opportunities for growth and improvement, and I am committed to continuous learning, striving to become better than I was yesterday.
